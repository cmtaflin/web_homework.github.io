There is a correlation relationship between the Max Temperature and Latitude, however more data evenly spread between -80 and 80 would be needed to better analyze the relationship of the full data set. This data set has its restrictions because a majority of the data is from 0-80 and only a small amount of data is negative with no data -60 to -80. Excluding -20 to 20 latitude, the data shows a lesser correlation with more outliers given the unbalance where the sample data is evenly spread within the range.


1.) There is a strong relationship close to the equator, 0, to the higher the Max Temperature between -20 to 20. The data is evenly spread with only a few outliers. Almost all of the data is has a max temp (F) of 25 to 75.

2.) The relationship from 20 to 80 latitude is not as strong as when closer to the equator. This data has many more outliers and the spread between the data points with the same latitude is significantly larger as the data gets farther from the equator.

3.) Given the limited data, the relationship from -20 to -80 seems like it could be similar to 20 to 80 latitude, but it would be a huge stretch to state that with any certain. The data in this range doesn't have a lot of data points, the range is only from -20 to about -60 latitude, and the outliers start closer to the equator than on the positive range from 20-80.
